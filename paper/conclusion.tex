\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

RAR presents a reconstruction-conditioned CIL framework for encrypted-traffic classification. It transforms per-flow reconstruction errors into historical-class compatibility scores and uses the same signal to weight the old-class distillation loss and new-class cross-path margin during training and to softly route predictions between old- and new-class paths during inference. The contribution lies in this traffic-specific integration for unified-label-space CIL rather than in reconstruction-based task inference itself. Experiments on six encrypted-traffic datasets show that RAR consistently reduces forgetting while improving mixed-class recognition performance over the evaluated classical CIL baselines.

Although effective, RAR still relies on limited replay samples to characterize the historical-class manifold and may face challenges when old and new classes exhibit highly similar representations. Future work will explore more adaptive compatibility signals and integrate reconstruction-aware routing with incremental representation learning to further enhance continual traffic classification.
